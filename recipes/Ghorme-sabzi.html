Iranian Main Dish

  • Ingredients:
  1. 400g lamb or beef, cubed
  2. 1 large onion, finely chopped
  3. 4 tbsp dried fenugreek leaves (shambalileh)
  4. 300g fresh herbs, finely chopped
  5. 1 ½ tbsp dried limes (limoo amani) or 2–3 whole dried limes
  6. 1 cup red kidney beans (or black-eyed beans), soaked overnight
  7. 1 tsp turmeric
  8. Salt and black pepper, to taste
  9. Oil
  10. Water

About Ghorme Sabzi

Ghorme Sabzi is one of the most iconic and beloved dishes in Iranian cuisine. Known for its rich aroma and deep green color, it is a stew made with fresh herbs, red kidney beans, and tender meat — usually lamb or beef. Its flavor is deepened with dried limes (limoo amani), giving it a tangy, slightly sour profile that’s uniquely Persian.

This dish is not only a staple in almost every household across Iran, but also a dish that evokes strong feelings of home and tradition. Every family has its own version, passed down through generations.

There are many regional variations of Ghorme Sabzi across Iran. For example:

  • Tehrani (Capital): Often includes more dried limes and a richer oil base.
  • Northern Iran:Sometimes includes additional herbs or garlic.
  • Southern Iran: Tends to be spicier and uses local herbs and spices.
